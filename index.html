Lab Works

Algae Cultivation

Algae cultivation under different light sources significantly affects their growth rate, pigment production, and biochemical composition. Exposure to red and blue LED lights often enhances photosynthetic efficiency and biomass yield compared to white or fluorescent lighting.

Dark Fermentation

Dark fermentation is an anaerobic process where microorganisms break down organic substrates to produce hydrogen and other by-products without the need for light. It offers a sustainable method for biohydrogen production using various organic wastes.

Flat Bed and Bubble Column Photobioreactor

Flat panel and bubble column photobioreactors are commonly used for algae cultivation due to their efficient light distribution and gas exchange. Flat panels offer high surface-to-volume ratios, while bubble columns ensure effective mixing through air sparging.

Microbial Fuel Cell

Microbial fuel cells (MFCs) generate electricity by harnessing the metabolic activity of microbes that oxidize organic matter. They offer a sustainable approach for simultaneous wastewater treatment and renewable energy production.

Microbial Desalination Cell

Microbial desalination cells (MDCs) use electroactive bacteria to remove salts from saline water while simultaneously generating electricity. They present a promising eco-friendly solution for water desalination and energy recovery.

Raceway Pond

A raceway pond is a shallow, open-channel bioreactor designed in a looped track format for large-scale algae cultivation. It uses paddle wheels to circulate water and ensure uniform light and nutrient distribution.

Microbial Electrolysis Cell

Microbial electrolysis cells (MECs) utilize electroactive microbes and a small external voltage to produce hydrogen from organic waste. They offer a sustainable approach for simultaneous waste treatment and renewable hydrogen generation.
